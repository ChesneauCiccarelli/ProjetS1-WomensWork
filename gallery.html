Gallery

Here you can discover other figures who have revolutionized the world and feminist ideology. Feel free to learn more about these stories that have changed the way people think. But first, some important dates on the ideology.

1791

Olympe de Gouges publishes the "Declaration of the Rights of Women and the Citizen".

29 April 1945

Women voted for the first time in France.

27 October 1946

Equality between men and women is written into the Preamble of the Constitution.

November 1947

First woman minister in France, Germaine Poinso-Chapuis.

13 July 1965

Women can exercise a professional activity without their husband's consent.

28 December 1967

Authorisation of contraception thanks to the Neuwirth law.

4 June 1970

The law on joint parental authority modifies the civil code and replaces paternal power with joint parental authority.

22 December 1972

A law establishes the principle of equal pay for men and women.

July 1974

Creation of the Secretary of State for the Status of Women.

17 January 1975

Promulgation of the law authorising the voluntary interruption of pregnancy (IVG), the Veil law.

31 December 1979

Law on abortion making definitive the provisions of the 1975 law and removing in particular certain obstacles to the performance of abortion, concerning the terms of the doctor's agreement and reception in hospital services.

23 December 1980

Rape is recognised as a crime.

8 March 1982

First National Women's Day.

13 July 1983

The Roudy law establishes professional equality between women and men.

4 July 2001

Act No. 2001-588 increases the legal time limit for recourse to abortion from 10 to 12 weeks.

24 March 2005

Presentation to the Council of Ministers of a bill on equal pay for women and men.

25 March 2013

A decree establishes 100% reimbursement of abortion as well as free medical contraceptives for minors aged over 15.

Figures of feminism

Olympe de Gouges

(1748 - 1793)

  • French woman of letters, who became a politician.
  • Considered one of the pioneers of feminism thanks to her publication in 1791 of the Declaration of the Rights of Women and the Citizen.
  • Died on the guillotine in Paris on 3 November 1793.

Learn more

Marie Curie

(1867 - 1934)

  • Polish physicist and chemist.
  • Won the Nobel Prize in Physics, along with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel for their research on radiation in 1903.
  • First woman to win the Nobel Prize and the only woman to win two.

Learn more

Rosa Parks

(1913 - 2005)

  • African-American woman / Emblematic figure in the fight against racial segregation in the United States.
  • Refused to give up her seat to a white passenger on the bus.
  • On 13 November 1956, the US Supreme Court struck down segregationist bus laws, declaring them unconstitutional.

Learn more

Simone Veil

(1927 - 2017)

  • Deported to Auschwitz at the age of 16 during the Holocaust, where she lost her entire family.
  • Appointed Minister of Health by President Valéry Giscard d'Estaing.
  • Passed the law decriminalizing a woman's recourse to voluntary termination of pregnancy (abortion).

Learn more

Simone De Beauvoir

(1908 - 1986)

  • French philosopher, novelist, memoirist and essayist.
  • Important feminist theorist: participated in the women's liberation movement in the 1970s.
  • « One is not born a woman: one becomes one. »

Learn more

Rebecca Amsellem

(1988)

  • French-Canadian entrepreneur, economist and feminist activist.
  • Creator of the feminist newsletter "Les Glorieuses"
  • Launched the #5november16h47 movement

Learn more

Madeleine Guilbert

(1910 - 2006)

  • French labour sociologist, feminist activist and trade unionist.
  • Participated in the Resistance during the Second World War.
  • Published three groundbreaking articles in La Revue Française du Travail on women's working conditions.

Learn more

Oprah Winfrey

(1957)

  • American television host and producer.
  • Helps girls assert themselves and claim who they are.
  • Launches the Oprah Winfrey Leadership Academy for Girls in South Africa. Her organisation educates disadvantaged girls aged 8 to 12.

Learn more

Tarana Burke

(1973)

  • Director of Programs at Girls for Gender Equity.
  • Started the #MeToo movement.

Learn more

Denis Mukwegey

(1955)

You don't have to be a woman to be a feminist.

  • Founder of the Panzi hospital. He has helped more than 40,000 women victims of extreme violence to escape from violence through medical care, education, social integration, legal support and other services.
  • Received the Nobel Peace Prize for helping thousands of women victims of rape to rebuild their lives physically and emotionally.
