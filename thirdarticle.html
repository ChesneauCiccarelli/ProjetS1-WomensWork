Women of power, a global job

Feminism raises issues that deserve our attention. Inequality is not only a French problem. From unrespected parity to wage inequality, the fight of feminist ideology seems to be global.

Insights on Europe

Although a developed country, France has a lot of progress to make in terms of parity. To fully understand a problem, it is important to educate oneself on the subject and to understand the context around it. For this, we need some basic knowledge as well as some figures. The book "Femmes et Hommes, Regards sur la parité" from INSEE references is perfect to understand the facts.

The National Institute of Statistics and Economic Studies (Institut national de la statistique et des études économiques - INSEE) is a Directorate General of the Ministry of the Economy and Finance. Its mission is to collect, analyse and disseminate information on the French economy and society throughout its territory.

This edition of the book brings us more specifically to the inequalities at the time of retirement today and for the generations that are of working age as well as to the distribution of family and domestic tasks between genders and the beneficiaries of the complement of free choice of activity. Each edition highlights a new and growing problem in our society. Keeping the thematic sheets of the previous years, it takes stock of the situation of parity in France but also in the world.

It raises the fact that the fight is not only French but also European. The wage gap raised in the article "From gendered work to wage inequality" is present in all countries of the European Union. Comparing the average gross hourly income, the country with the smallest gap is Luxembourg. Women's wages are lower than men's by 1.3%. This is a great achievement, but it still shows that equality is not present. On the other hand, the largest gap would be found in Estonia with a rate of 21.7%. France would be above the European average of 14.1% with a pay gap of 16.5%. Apart from the fact that these rates are unacceptable, it should be noted that the European average of pay inequality is slightly decreasing over the period 2011-2019.

Women of power

However, the data from our research must be interpreted with caution. The seeds of wage inequality are certainly numerous, but above all they differ from country to country. Differences in education, labour market segregation and the sharing of family and domestic work must obviously be taken into account. The historian and specialist in contemporary labor history Sylvie Schweitzer develops these ideas in her book "Femmes de pouvoir: une histoire de l'égalité professionnelle en Europe (XIXe-XXe siècle)".

Sylvie Schweitzer focused on French women and brings elements of comparison to other countries, from Western to Eastern Europe. By examining different professions such as law, public administration, medicine, journalism, politics, academic and judicial positions, the author makes the history of resistance to equality with men the central theme of her work. She also points out in this book the role of feminist lawyers specialized in gender equality in the workplace, in labor law, in women's civil law and in voting rights.

It is an interesting work, to discover the gender inequalities in Europe. It offers the arguments put forward to limit the advantages acquired by women and their refutation within society. It gathers the stories of women pioneers in the evolution in different fields of profession. Some of them you can find in the gallery of feminism. It highlights the development of gendered work explained in the article "From gendered work to wage inequality".

From personal struggle to the global feminist cause

It is not an exaggeration to say that total parity is not present in any country of the globe. The women of the world are oppressed and suffer every day the problems related to their condition as women. Women or men, fighting against inequalities is a work that is first of all personal. The commitment against machismo, misandry and sexism must be at the heart of a person's personal development. For the purpose of documentation each person must be able to think and reason. The fight against discrimination should be a priority in an institution that wants to be secular, fraternal, fair and egalitarian.

On the model of the #MeToo movement for example. This worldwide social movement encouraged women to speak out. This mainly on the theme of rape and sexual assault. A hashtag that broke down across the world in different forms depending on the country (#BalanceTonPorc in France). Initially launched by Tarana Burke (which you can see below) in 2007, an American activist, to denounce sexual violence, especially against minorities. On this model, women from all over the world have been able to raise their voices against harassment.

In the end, there is still a long way to go, but from the Amazons to #BalanceTonPorc, the powerful women of the world, and the sensible men, have made the world move, and will continue to shake up mentalities to one day reach equality as it should be, without segregation of gender identity, ethnicity, or anything else.
